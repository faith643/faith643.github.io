Abstract

In computer vision, using custom concepts to generate images that align with user-provided personalized concepts while still maintaining a natural integration with general concepts remains a key challenge. Current approaches struggle with two critical technical bottlenecks when addressing the issue of missing general concepts during generation with custom concepts: 1) Over-constraining the custom concept results in the loss of its personalized characteristics (generalization), and 2) the interference of custom concept coding in the background area leads to the generation of general concepts being affected by custom concepts. We propose MDA-Mask, combining a maximal divergent anchoring (MDA) constraint with a background leakage suppression (BLS) mechanism. We select three concepts with the largest semantic gaps as anchors to prevent over-constraining the custom concept and enable it to learn distinctive features. Additionally, it uses a mask-guided attention mechanism to suppress background leakage and minimize encoding interference. We assess performance by combining custom and general concepts. Compared to mainstream methods, the proposed approach improves frechet inception distance (FID), text alignment, and image alignment metrics by 4.3%, 5.2%, and 3.1%, respectively, markedly enhancing concept composability.
